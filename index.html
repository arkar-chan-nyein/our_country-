Welcome To Myanmar

Overview

Myanmar, also known as Burma, is a beautiful country located in Southeast Asia. It shares borders with Thailand, Laos, China, India, and Bangladesh. The country is famous for its ancient temples, rich culture, and natural beauty.

Culture

Myanmar has a unique culture based on Buddhism, which is practiced by the majority of its people. Traditional festivals such as Thingyan (Water Festival) and Thadingyut (Festival of Lights) are celebrated throughout the country with joy and excitement.

Famous Places in Myanmar

  • Bagan: An ancient city with over 2,000 Buddhist temples, pagodas, and monasteries built between the 11th and 13th centuries.
  • Shwedagon Pagoda: The most sacred Buddhist pagoda in Myanmar, located in Yangon and covered with gold plates.
  • Inle Lake: Known for its floating villages, floating gardens, and leg-rowing fishermen.
  • Mandalay Hill: A famous viewpoint for watching sunsets and exploring Buddhist monasteries.
  • Kyaiktiyo Pagoda (Golden Rock): A small pagoda built atop a huge granite boulder covered with gold leaf, balancing on a cliff edge.
  • Ngapali Beach: A beautiful white sandy beach on the Bay of Bengal, famous for its clear water and relaxing atmosphere.

Famous Food in Myanmar

  • Mohinga: Myanmar 's national dish, a rice noodle and fish soup served with crispy fritters, boiled egg, banana stem, and lime.
  • Lahpet Thoke (Tea Leaf Salad): A unique salad made from fermented tea leaves mixed with fried beans, peanuts, sesame seeds, garlic, and tomatoes.
  • Shan Noodles: A popular noodle dish from Shan State, served either as a soup or dry noodles with chicken or pork, peanuts, and pickled vegetables.
  • Ohn No Khao Swe: A rich coconut milk noodle soup with chicken, similar to Thai Khao Soi, served with crispy noodles on top and lime.
  • Nan Gyi Thoke: A thick noodle salad mixed with chicken curry gravy, boiled egg, and crunchy toppings, usually eaten as a breakfast dish.
  • Mont Lin Ma Yar: A famous street snack of tiny crispy rice flour pancakes filled with quail egg, peas, and spring onions.

Union People of Myanmar

Myanmar is home to over 135 distinct ethnic groups. The country is proudly known as the Union of Myanmar because of its rich ethnic diversity. Each group has its own traditions, languages, cultures, and customs. Major ethnic groups include:

  • Bamar: The largest ethnic group, making up around 68% of the population. Most live in central Myanmar.
  • Shan: An ethnic group from the eastern highlands, known for their own language, culture, and famous Shan cuisine.
  • Kayin (Karen): One of the major ethnic groups, mainly living in Kayin State and known for their beautiful traditional dress and Karen songs.
  • Rakhine: An ethnic group from the western Rakhine State, with their own traditions and coastal lifestyle.
  • Chin: Living in Chin State, known for their beautiful mountainous homeland and unique customs.
  • Mon: One of Myanmar 's earliest settlers, living mainly in Mon State with a rich Buddhist history.
  • Kachin: From the northernmost region, known for their colorful Kachin Manaw Festival and bravery.
  • Kayah: Known for the famous long-necked women and their distinctive traditional dress and music.
